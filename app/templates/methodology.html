Technical Methodology

Overview

The CVI Risk Predictor employs a multi-model machine learning approach to assess neighborhood-level violence risk. Our methodology integrates diverse data sources and applies advanced predictive analytics while maintaining strict ethical safeguards.

Data Sources

Data Category Source Update Frequency
Crime Incidents City Open Data Portals Daily
Socioeconomic Data U.S. Census Bureau (ACS) Annually
Social Vulnerability CDC Social Vulnerability Index Every 2 years
Environmental Data 311 Service Requests, OpenStreetMap Daily

Machine Learning Models

1. Logistic Regression

Purpose: Interpretable baseline model for understanding risk factors

Performance:

Accuracy: 68.5% Precision: 76.1% Recall: 68.5% F1-Score: 71.0%

Use Case: Explaining "why" a neighborhood is flagged as high-risk through transparent coefficients

2. Random Forest Classifier

Purpose: High-accuracy predictions capturing complex patterns

Performance:

Accuracy: 81.0% Precision: 79.7% Recall: 81.0% F1-Score: 80.2%

Use Case: Primary operational model for resource allocation decisions

3. K-Means Clustering

Purpose: Group neighborhoods into risk tiers

Risk Tiers:

Low Risk Moderate Risk High Risk Critical Risk

Use Case: Long-term strategic planning and citywide overview

Feature Engineering

The models use 15 key features aggregated at the neighborhood-week level:

Temporal Features
  • Lagged crime counts (1, 2, 4 weeks prior)
  • Rolling averages (4-week window)
  • Trend slopes (12-week linear regression)
Socioeconomic Features
  • Median household income
  • Unemployment rate
  • Poverty rate
  • Educational attainment (% less than high school)
Vulnerability Features
  • CDC Social Vulnerability Index (overall and by theme)
  • Housing vacancy rate
Environmental Features
  • Abandoned building density
  • Population density

Model Training & Validation

Models are trained on historical data using an 80/20 train-test split with stratified sampling to ensure balanced class representation. All models undergo 5-fold cross-validation to ensure generalizability.

The target variable is binary: neighborhoods with violent crime counts exceeding the 75th percentile are classified as "high-risk" (1), while others are "low-risk" (0).

Ethical Safeguards

  • Aggregation: All analysis at neighborhood level only (minimum population: 500)
  • Transparency: Interpretable models with explainable predictions
  • Bias Documentation: Clear acknowledgment of data limitations
  • Prevention Focus: Designed for CVI organizations, not law enforcement
  • Privacy Protection: No personal identifiers or individual-level predictions

Limitations

This tool has important limitations that users must understand:

  • Crime data reflects reported incidents, which may be influenced by policing patterns and community trust
  • Predictions are probabilistic and cannot account for all factors influencing violence
  • The model is a decision-support tool and should augment, not replace, professional judgment
  • Performance metrics are based on historical data and may vary in real-world application
